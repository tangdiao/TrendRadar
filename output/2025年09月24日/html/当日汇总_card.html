This initiative aligns with Beijing’s broader goal of reducing dependence on the US dollar and Western financial hubs such as London and Switzerland. China has also eased restrictions on gold imports and launched offshore vaults and yuan-denominated gold contracts in Hong Kong to internationalize its currency and increase trading volumes. This move could attract nations looking to secure reserves and mitigate risks of financial isolation, particularly after the US and allies froze Russia’s foreign exchange reserves in 2022.
